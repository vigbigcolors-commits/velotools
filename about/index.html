How "no upload" actually works

Most online tools work by sending your file to a server, processing it there, and sending the result back. That round trip is where privacy risk, slow uploads, and ongoing server costs all come from. VeloTools removes that step entirely.

Every tool is built on standard browser APIs that have existed for years but are rarely used to their full potential: the Canvas API for image decoding, re-compression, resizing, and pixel-level effects; the File API for reading bytes directly from disk; WebAssembly for running AI models (background removal) and PDF processing at near-native speed; and pure JavaScript for parsing file formats — including a hand-written TIFF/IFD walker that extracts the embedded JPEG preview from camera RAW files.

The practical result: open a tool, drop a file, and the entire operation — decode, process, re-encode — happens in your device's memory in milliseconds. You can disconnect from the internet after the page loads and every tool keeps working.

Zero-knowledge by design

There is no upload endpoint for your files to begin with — not "we delete it after," but "it was never sent." Screenshots, IDs, client photos, and invoices stay on your device.

Instant, with no queue

No upload progress bar because there's no upload. Processing starts the moment you choose a file and finishes in under a second for most images.

Works offline

Once a tool's page has loaded, you can disconnect from the internet entirely. The compressor, converter, and background remover keep functioning without a connection.

Free forever — here's why that's sustainable

Server-based tools charge subscriptions partly because every file you upload costs them compute, storage, and bandwidth. VeloTools has almost none of those costs: your browser does the processing, so the marginal cost of one more user — or one more 15-image batch — is effectively zero.

That's why every feature is unlocked for everyone: no quotas, no "pro" tier, no watermarks, no daily limits. The site is supported by minimal, non-intrusive advertising — enough to cover hosting and development without needing to gate features behind a paywall.
