Under the hood

State-of-the-art AI technology
running entirely in your browser

We use the same neural network category as commercial server-side APIs — ISNet salient object detection — compiled to ONNX and executed by Microsoft’s ONNX Runtime Web. No shortcuts, no lesser model. Just a different execution location: your device instead of our servers.

Neural Network

ISNet Salient Detection

ISNet (IS-Net) achieved state-of-the-art results on DIS (Dichotomous Image Segmentation) benchmarks. It detects foreground objects at multiple spatial resolutions simultaneously — this is why it accurately handles both the broad body outline and individual thin hair strands in a single forward pass.

GPU Execution

WebGPU + ONNX Runtime Web

The network is exported to ONNX format and run by Microsoft’s ONNX Runtime Web. On Chrome/Edge 113+ and Android Chrome 121+, inference runs on your GPU via WebGPU — achieving 16–20× the throughput of single-threaded CPU execution. Older browsers fall back to WASM automatically.

Edge Algorithm

Local Alpha Matting

After the AI produces a coarse mask, we run a local alpha matting pass on every edge pixel. Each semi-transparent pixel is re-estimated by projecting its color onto the line between nearby confirmed foreground and background samples — recovering individual whiskers and hair tips the hard segmentation clips.

Post-Processing

Color Decontamination

AI cutouts often have a semi-transparent “halo” of the original background color bleeding into edge pixels. Our decontamination pass samples nearby high-opacity pixels and blends edge colors away from the background. Invisible on white backgrounds; completely eliminates the halo effect on dark or colored backgrounds.

Source image quality

The photos that produce flawless
AI cutouts every time

The AI is only as good as the image you give it. These four factors make the difference between a result that’s 85% accurate (requires significant manual cleanup) and one that’s 98% accurate (export-ready with zero touch-up). Understanding them takes 2 minutes and will permanently improve your results.

01

High contrast between subject and background

The single most important factor. A person against a plain grey wall produces near-perfect results. The same person against dense green foliage requires significant manual editing. The AI detects foreground by learning what “belongs together” — when subject and background share color or texture, it’s genuinely harder to separate them. For product photography: a white lightbox with a white background will always beat a casual desk shot. For portraits: solid-color walls, studio backdrops, or open sky always outperform patterned interiors.

02

Sharp focus at the subject’s edges

Blurry subject edges produce blurry AI masks — the model can’t place the boundary if the pixels themselves are ambiguous. A portrait taken at f/1.4 with the face tack-sharp but the shoulders slightly soft will have excellent face edges and mediocre shoulder edges. For product photography: use f/8 or higher to keep all edges of the product in focus. For portraits: focus on the near eye and use at least f/2.8 to keep both eyes and the full head sharp. The AI will deliver a correspondingly sharp, clean mask.

03

Even, diffuse lighting without harsh shadows

Strong directional light creates hard shadows on the background that look like part of the subject to the AI. A product photographed with a single desk lamp creates shadows the model must guess about. Shoot products with two softboxes at 45° or in open shade outdoors. For portraits, avoid direct noon sun (harsh shadows) and prefer golden-hour or overcast light. Rim lighting — a subtle light behind the subject — actually helps by creating a bright separation line the model uses as an edge cue. Avoid subject shadows falling onto the background at all costs.

04

Fill the frame — subject close, tight composition

When the subject fills 60–80% of the frame, the AI has more resolution to work with at the edges. A product in the center of a very wide shot gives the model only 50 pixels of edge data to analyze. Zoom in so the subject nearly fills the frame. For portraits: shoot from the chest up rather than full-body from distance. For products: frame tightly with 5–10% breathing room. If you need a full-body shot or wide frame, that’s fine — but plan for 1–2 extra Refine button clicks to clean the finer edge detail the AI’s limited resolution misses.

Output formats explained

PNG, JPG, or WebP —
which format is right for you?

Choosing the wrong export format is the most common mistake after a perfect cutout. PNG on a website bloats page size. JPG for design work loses transparency. WebP in Photoshop won’t open. Here’s exactly when to use each — with zero ambiguity.

.PNG

Transparent & Lossless

PNG preserves the transparent alpha channel exactly as edited. Every edge pixel with partial transparency — including hair strands and soft edges — is stored precisely. File size is larger than JPG or WebP, but quality is mathematically perfect: no compression artifacts, no color shift. PNG is the only format that supports true transparency across all design tools.

Use PNG when the image will be placed on a colored or image background in Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, Canva, or print. Use PNG when the subject needs to stay usable on any background without visible fringing. Use PNG for all further editing — it’s the “master” export you work from.

✓ Best for: Design work & transparency
.JPG

Compressed & Universal

JPG exports a flat image with a solid background — white by default, or any background color you select in the background panel. No transparency, but far smaller file sizes than PNG. The compression is perceptually tuned: at 95% quality (the VeloTools default), the difference from lossless is invisible at normal viewing sizes. JPG opens everywhere, including legacy e-commerce upload systems that reject PNG or WebP.

Use JPG white for Amazon, Etsy, Shopify, and any marketplace that requires a white background. Use JPG with a custom background when delivering mockups or lifestyle shots to clients who need the background baked in. Use JPG for LinkedIn headshots, CV photos, and any context where the file will be emailed or printed directly.

.WebP

Smallest File & Modern

WebP is a modern format developed by Google that achieves 25–35% smaller file sizes than JPG and 20–30% smaller than PNG at equivalent visual quality. Crucially, WebP supports full transparency — so it can replace PNG in web contexts with a massive file-size advantage. All modern browsers support WebP (Chrome, Firefox, Safari 14+, Edge). The smaller file size directly improves Core Web Vitals scores, LCP time, and Google PageSpeed Insights ratings — real SEO impact.

Use WebP for any image that will appear on a public website, blog, or web store product page. Avoid WebP when the file needs to be opened in Photoshop CS or older versions, or sent to clients who may use legacy software. For everything web-facing: WebP first, always.

Expert technique

How to get perfect cutouts
every single time

The AI handles 90% of the work in seconds. These four techniques — the standard workflow at professional e-commerce retouching agencies worldwide — handle the remaining 10% that separates a good cutout from a flawless one. Each technique takes under 5 minutes once learned.

01

Always inspect edges at 200–400% zoom before exporting

Use the scroll wheel or zoom buttons to reach 200–400% and trace the full perimeter of your subject carefully. Look for: white or colored halo fringe at hair edges, missing fine strands or whiskers, jagged staircase transitions on diagonal edges, and any dark semi-transparent spots in the background area. The Refine button runs an additional Gaussian erosion pass that cleans persistent halos in one click. The Clean button removes any isolated background specks. This 60-second inspection step is what separates a 3-minute from a 30-minute result — and a professional-looking result from an amateur one.

02

Use Smart Erase first on any solid-color background

If your background is a single color — studio white, sky blue, green screen, product photography grey — click once with Smart Erase directly on the background area. It performs a connected BFS flood-fill that removes all similarly-colored pixels connected to the click point in a single operation. This is dramatically faster and more consistent than manually brushing. The contiguous-only algorithm means it won’t accidentally remove skin tones that happen to match the background color elsewhere in the image. For gradient or complex backgrounds, try clicking multiple areas to remove distinct color zones.

03

Two-pass technique for complex hair, fur, and fine edges

When the AI has removed too much: paint back with the Restore brush at 35–50% opacity — build up gradually rather than at full strength. Then use the Erase brush at 15–25% opacity to remove the background-color halo that reappears at restored edges. Alternate between these two passes 2–3 times until the edge looks natural at 300% zoom. This restore-then-erase iterative technique is the professional standard for portrait, pet, and product photography retouching. At 100% opacity you get hard edges; at 20–40% opacity you get the soft semi-transparency that makes hair look natural.

04

Test on a dark background before final export

Before exporting, click the dark navy swatch in the Background panel and check for halo fringing. The Refine button removes fringe along the border. Nearly all cutout defects are invisible on white but immediately visible on dark or saturated backgrounds. A result that looks perfect on white may have a 2px white halo that turns into a distracting glow on dark blue. Fix the halo now — it takes 30 seconds — and your cutout becomes truly universal: usable on any background without further adjustment. Then switch back to transparent for the final PNG export.

Frequently asked questions

Does this tool really work without uploading my image?
Yes — 100%. The entire background removal process happens in your browser using WebAssembly and (on supported hardware) WebGPU. Your image pixels never leave your device — ever. You can disconnect from the internet after the page loads and the tool still works perfectly. The AI model executes entirely within your browser’s JavaScript sandbox. We have no server infrastructure for image processing, no S3 bucket to leak from, and no database storing your images.
What happens to my image data after processing?
Nothing — because the image never leaves your device. All pixel data (both the original image and the computed alpha mask) lives in your browser’s JavaScript memory. When you close the browser tab, that memory is released by the OS. We collect zero telemetry on image content, dimensions, or processing results. There are no cookies, no tracking pixels, and no analytics SDKs that could indirectly capture image metadata. This is by design — the architecture makes data collection structurally impossible, not just a policy decision.
How does the AI quality compare to remove.bg or Photoshop AI?
For most subjects — people, animals, and products against clear backgrounds — the initial AI quality is comparable to remove.bg and better than Canva’s background remover. We use ISNet, the same category of state-of-the-art salient object detection model used by commercial tools. For challenging subjects like fine hair against a busy background, the manual tools (Erase, Restore, Refine) let you match or exceed Photoshop AI results with full control. The key advantage over Photoshop: everything runs in the browser, free, with no subscription.
Why is the first image slower than subsequent ones?
The first time you use the tool, the AI model (~40MB) downloads from the CDN and is cached permanently in your browser. This typically takes 10–30 seconds depending on your connection speed. Every subsequent image processes in 1–5 seconds from cache without any download. The model stays cached indefinitely — you only pay the download cost once per browser profile, and it survives browser restarts.
What image formats are supported for input and export?
Input: JPG, JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF (first frame), BMP, TIFF, AVIF, HEIC (on supported browsers), SVG, and ICO. You can also paste directly with Ctrl+V. Export: PNG (transparent, lossless — ideal for Figma, Photoshop, Canva and any design tool), JPG on white (Amazon/Etsy/Shopify standard), JPG with custom background color (client mockups), and WebP (smallest file size for web publishing and PageSpeed optimization). PNG is recommended for any further editing; WebP for anything going live on the web.

How do I get the best results on curly hair and fur?
Start with the Refine button in the toolbar — this runs an enhanced Gaussian erosion pass plus alpha matting to recover thin strands. Then zoom to 300% and inspect edges. Use the Restore brush at 40% opacity to paint back any hair tips the AI removed. Use the Erase brush at 15% opacity to clean fringing at restored areas. Alternate between these two passes. For complex curly hair: 3–7 minutes total. For simple fur or short hair: Refine button once, then a single Erase pass at 20% opacity along the edge, 60–90 seconds total. The key is patience at 200%+ zoom — the tool has the precision, you just need to use it.

Yes — fully optimized for touch devices. Pinch two fingers on the canvas to zoom in for precision editing. Works on iPad Pro and the latest Safari on iOS 18+.
How does it handle semi-transparent objects like glass, smoke, or water?
Semi-transparent objects are the hardest case for any background removal tool. ISNet was trained primarily on solid opaque subjects and does not reliably separate glass from background. For wine glasses, smoke, and water splashes: the AI will give a partial result, typically preserving the solid parts and partially removing the transparent areas. Use the Restore brush at low opacity (20–30%) to carefully paint back semi-transparent regions. The alpha matting system correctly preserves partial transparency — so if you restore a glass rim, it will appear genuinely semi-transparent in the PNG export, not hard-edged. For professional glass product photography, we recommend starting with a professional clipping path service for the outer shape, then using VeloTools for edge refinement.
