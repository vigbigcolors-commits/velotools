How our AI background removal works

Understanding the technology helps you get the best possible results. Here's exactly what happens when you upload an image.

01

The AI model loads once, runs forever

On your first use, a neural network model downloads to your browser cache. Every future use is instant. The model was trained on millions of images and understands the difference between foreground and background at a semantic level — not just by color.

02

Alpha matting handles complex edges

Our AI calculates partial transparency at every edge pixel using alpha matting. This is how hair, fur, and semi-transparent objects look natural instead of hard-edged and artificial.

03

Manual refinement perfects the result

AI handles roughly 90% of the work. The remaining 10% — missed strands, ambiguous edges — is where the manual tools come in. The hair brush, feather, and 5× magnifier let you verify and perfect every detail.

04

Export in the exact format you need

PNG preserves transparency for Photoshop, Figma, and Canva. JPG with a white background is perfect for e-commerce. WebP balances quality and tiny file size for fast websites.

Pro tips for perfect cutouts

Getting professional results from an AI background remover is part technique. These methods are used by retouchers at e-commerce agencies worldwide.

💡

Start with good source photos

High contrast between subject and background gives the AI more to work with. A person against a plain wall is always easier than the same person against dense foliage.

🔍

Always check at 5× zoom

Use the Magnifier before downloading. Problems invisible at full view — halo fringing, missed strands — become obvious when zoomed in. This single step separates amateur from pro.

💁

Use the Hair brush for strands

For fine hair, switch to the Hair brush and reduce opacity to 40-60%. Paint along the edge to create natural-looking semi-transparent hair tips instead of a hard cut.

🌊

Feather soft subjects

For fluffy animals, smoke, or fabric edges, apply Feather after removal. This softly blurs the mask edge and removes the harsh "cut out" look entirely.
