How our AI background removal works

Understanding the technology helps you get the best possible results. Here's exactly what happens when you upload an image.

01

The AI model loads once, runs forever

On your first use, a neural network model downloads to your browser cache. Every future use is instant. The model understands the difference between foreground and background at a semantic level — not just by color.

02

Alpha matting handles complex edges

Our AI calculates partial transparency at every edge pixel. This is how hair, fur, and semi-transparent objects look natural instead of hard-edged and artificial.

03

Edge decontamination removes the halo

After AI removal we run a color decontamination pass — eliminating the thin semi-transparent halo that betrays a bad cutout when placed on a dark background.

04

Export in the exact format you need

PNG preserves transparency for Photoshop, Figma, and Canva. JPG with a white background is perfect for e-commerce. WebP balances quality and tiny file size for fast websites.

Pro tips for perfect cutouts

Getting professional results from an AI background remover is part technique. These methods are used by retouchers at e-commerce agencies worldwide.

💡

Start with good source photos

High contrast between subject and background gives the AI more to work with. A person against a plain wall is always easier than the same person against dense foliage.

🔍

Use scroll-to-zoom on edges

Scroll wheel to zoom in on any edge area. Inspect for halo fringing or missed detail. This step separates amateur from pro results — always check at 300% zoom.

🌟

Use Smart Erase for solid backgrounds

Click once on any background color with Smart Erase — it removes all similar-colored pixels in one shot. Perfect for studio shots, product photos, and flat backgrounds.

🌊

Restore then re-erase for fine edges

Use Restore brush at low opacity to bring back semi-transparent hair tips, then Soft Erase to clean what shouldn't be there. Two-pass technique gives the cleanest edges.

Yes — 100%. The entire background removal process happens in your browser using WebAssembly. Your image pixels never leave your device. You can even disconnect from the internet after the page loads and it still works perfectly.
Is it really free? What's the catch?
It's genuinely free forever — no catch. No signup, no watermarks, no daily limits, no subscription. We built it as part of the VeloTools suite of free browser-based utilities.
What image formats are supported?
We support JPG, JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF (first frame), BMP, TIFF, AVIF, HEIC (on supported browsers), SVG, and ICO. If your format isn't listed, convert it to PNG first using any free converter.
Can I use the results commercially?
Yes. Your edited images are entirely yours — no watermarks, no licensing restrictions. Use them for e-commerce listings, marketing, client work, or anything else without attribution.
Is there a limit on image size or quantity?
No limits at all. Process as many images as you want, as large as your device memory allows. No credits, no daily caps, no subscription required. The only limit is your device's RAM.
Why is the first processing slower than the second?
The first time you use the tool, the AI model (~80MB) downloads and caches in your browser. Every subsequent image processes instantly from cache. You only wait once — ever.
How do I get the best results on hair and fur?
After the AI pass, zoom in to 200–400% and use Soft Erase at 30–50% opacity along the edge to clean fringing. Then use the Restore brush to bring back any hair tips the AI missed. The Refine button also runs an additional alpha matting pass to recover thin strands automatically.
