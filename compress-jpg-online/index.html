Why compress JPEG files?

Large JPG photos slow down websites, fill up email inboxes, and hit upload limits on forms and marketplaces. Compressing before you publish keeps quality high while cutting file size dramatically — better Core Web Vitals, faster page loads, and happier visitors.

Tips for best JPEG compression

  • 80–85% quality is the sweet spot for photos — smaller files, same look.
  • For web, convert to WebP after compressing — often 30% smaller than JPEG at the same quality.
  • Resize oversized camera photos (4000×3000) to the display size you actually need before compressing.
  • Already tiny JPEGs (<50KB) may not shrink much — they are already heavily compressed.
