Your files never leave your device.

Most online compressors work by uploading your image to a remote server, processing it there, and sending it back. That means your private photos briefly live on someone else's hardware — and you simply have to trust they're deleted.

VeloTools takes a fundamentally different approach. The entire engine runs as JavaScript in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. When you drop an image, it's decoded, re-compressed, and re-encoded entirely in your device's memory. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is logged, and no server ever sees your file.

The result is the rare combination of speed, privacy, and zero cost — no upload wait, no data exposure, no subscription. You can even disconnect from the internet once the page has loaded and keep working.

Next-gen formats. Maximum savings.

The same photo, encoded three ways. Newer formats pack far more quality into far fewer bytes — which means faster pages and lower bandwidth bills.

JPEGUniversal
Baseline size100%

Works everywhere. The safe default for photographs, but the least efficient of the three. No transparency support.

WebPBest for web
Typical size~32% smaller

Google's modern format. 25–40% smaller than JPEG at equal quality, with full alpha transparency. Supported in every modern browser.

AVIFSmallest
Typical size~52% smaller

The newest standard. Up to 50% smaller than JPEG with HDR support and transparency. Ideal when every kilobyte counts.

PNG is always lossless, so the quality slider has no effect on it — to shrink a PNG, convert it to WebP or AVIF and keep full transparency. For lossy formats (JPG, WebP, AVIF) you control the exact quality, with 80% being the sweet spot where files are dramatically smaller yet visually identical.
