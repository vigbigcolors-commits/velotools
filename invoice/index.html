Best Practices

How to write an invoice that gets paid fast

A professional invoice isn't just a payment request — it's a reflection of your business. These steps ensure clarity, trust, and on-time payment.

01

Use a unique invoice number

Every invoice needs a sequential, unique number (INV-001, INV-002…). This makes tracking, accounting, and dispute resolution straightforward for both parties. Never repeat numbers.

02

Be specific in your line items

Vague descriptions cause payment delays. Instead of "Design work", write "Homepage UI redesign — 3 revisions (12 hrs × $95/hr)". The more precise, the fewer questions and the faster the approval.

03

Always include a due date

Invoices without due dates get deprioritised. Net 30 is the global standard, but for smaller projects or new clients, Net 7 or Net 14 protects your cash flow significantly.

04

State your late payment terms

A late payment clause (e.g. "1.5% monthly interest on overdue balances") dramatically reduces payment delays without damaging client relationships. It's standard practice globally.

05

Include every payment method

The easier you make it to pay, the faster you get paid. List bank transfer details, your PayPal email, Wise handle, and any other method you accept — directly in the invoice footer.

06

Match your invoice to your brand

A consistent, professional invoice increases perceived value and trust. Use your logo, brand colours, and a template that reflects the quality of your work. Your invoice is your last impression.

Glossary

Invoice terms every freelancer should know

Understanding standard invoice terminology makes you look more professional and avoids costly misunderstandings with clients and accountants.

Net 30Payment is due 30 calendar days from the invoice date. The most common B2B payment term globally.
PO NumberPurchase Order number assigned by your client. Including it speeds up their accounts payable process and reduces payment delays.
VAT / GSTValue Added Tax or Goods and Services Tax. If you're registered for VAT/GST in your country, you must include your tax number and charge the applicable rate.
SubtotalThe total of all line items before any discounts, taxes, or shipping charges are applied.
RetainerA recurring monthly fee paid in advance for an ongoing service commitment. Often invoiced as a single line item.
Due on ReceiptPayment is expected immediately upon receiving the invoice. Common for one-time purchases and first-time clients.

Yes — free forever. No signup required, no watermarks on your invoices, no subscription, no hidden limits. We will never add a watermark to any invoice you create.
How do I export my invoice as PDF?
Click "Download PDF". Your browser's native print dialog opens — select "Save as PDF" as the destination printer. This produces perfect A4 output at print quality, not a screenshot.
Is my invoice data stored anywhere?
Everything is saved in your browser's local storage on your device only. Nothing is sent to our servers. Your client names, pricing, and bank details stay completely private.
Can I add my company logo?
Yes. Upload a PNG, JPG, or SVG logo in the "Your Info" tab. It appears in the invoice preview immediately and is included at full resolution in both PDF and PNG exports.
How do I add multiple tax rates?
In the Items tab, Tax 1 is always visible. Click "Add second tax" to reveal Tax 2. You can name both (e.g. "VAT" and "Local Tax") and set independent rates. Both appear as separate lines in the totals.

20+ currencies including USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, JPY, CHF, AED, SAR, INR, BRL, MXN, SGD, HKD, RUB, CNY, AMD and more. The correct symbol is applied to every number automatically.
