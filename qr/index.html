8 QR code types explained

Different QR types encode different data formats optimized for each use case. Choose the right type for the best scanning experience.

URL

Website links

The most common type. Encodes a URL — opens directly in the phone's browser on scan. Always include https:// for best compatibility.

WiFi

Network credentials

Scan to connect to WiFi automatically — no password typing required. Supports WPA/WPA2, WEP, and open networks. Perfect for cafés, hotels, offices.

vCard

Digital business card

Scan to save a complete contact — name, phone, email, organization, website, and address. Opens the native contacts app on any smartphone.

Email

Pre-filled email

Scan opens a new email with address, subject, and body pre-filled. Great for customer support, feedback forms, or event registration.

SMS

Pre-filled text message

Opens the messaging app with a number and optional pre-written message. Useful for SMS marketing opt-ins and quick contact.

Location

GPS coordinates

Opens Google Maps (or native maps app) to a specific lat/lng location. Perfect for events, storefronts, or anything with an address.

Pro tips for scannable QR codes

A beautiful QR code that can't be scanned is useless. Follow these rules to keep your QR functional while looking great.

💡

Keep contrast high

Foreground should always be darker than background (or vice versa). A contrast ratio below 3:1 causes scan failures on older devices.

🌟

Use H error correction with logos

Error correction H allows 30% of the QR to be covered — exactly what a center logo does. Always use H when adding a logo.

🔍

Test before printing

Always scan your QR code with at least two different phones before printing. Download the SVG for sharp edges at any print size.

📐

Keep URLs short

Shorter URLs = simpler QR patterns = more reliable scans. Use a URL shortener for long links — this also enables scan analytics.

Frequently asked questions

Are these QR codes free to use commercially?
Yes, completely free for any commercial use — product packaging, marketing materials, business cards, menus, anything. No watermarks, no attribution required.
Do QR codes expire?
Static QR codes (what we generate here) never expire. They're just encoded data — as long as the destination URL exists and the QR is readable, it works forever.
What's the minimum print size for a QR code?
Minimum recommended size is 2×2 cm (0.8 inch) for scanning at arm's length. For larger scan distances (e.g. posters), scale proportionally — 3 meters away needs roughly 15×15 cm.
Why use SVG instead of PNG?
SVG is vector — it's infinitely scalable with no pixelation. Use SVG for print, signage, and anything larger than a business card. Use PNG for digital use (websites, social media, email).
Can I add a logo to my QR code?
Yes. Upload any image in the Logo section. We recommend keeping it under 30% of the QR size and using H error correction (already selected by default) so the QR remains scannable.
